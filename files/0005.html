Simple problems involving only forces

      Here you can find four solved problems that can be used to practice simple static situations. The problems are not mine, but the solutions are.

      Problem 1: A boat towed by two tugboats.

      This problem requires you to understand that statics means zero acceleration, not necessarily zero velocity. You will see that the boat, due to the water drag, travels at constant speed, and this also requires that the sum of forces is equal to zero. In architecture, it is usual to assume that velocity is zero as well, but strictly speaking, a constant velocity system is also static.
